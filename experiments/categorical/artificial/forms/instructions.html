Guess My Object!

In this experiment you will play a fun communication game with another turker! In this experiment, you will encounter eight objects:

One of you will be assigned the Speaker role and the other will be the Listener . On each round, we will pick four of these eight objects and show them to both of you. The objects will be shown in different orders on your different screens. One of the four is the target, which is outlined in black. Only the Speaker can see which object is the target.

The goal of the Speaker is to send a message so that the Listener can figure out which object is the target. You'll both get a bonus of $0.03 for each correct response, so pay attention!

The goal of the Listener is to identify the target based on what the Speaker has told you. If you are the Listener, you will first need to wait until the Speaker has sent their message before being able to select an object.

What makes this game more interesting and challenging is the content of your messages: instead of using the English language, you get to make up a language together! Both you and your partner will see an array of nine messages that could be part of this language: to send one of the messages, the Speaker can click and drag it into the grey 'dropzone' below.

Once the Listener clicks on the object they believe is the target, both players will be given feedback: the Speaker will see what the Listener clicked, and the Listener will see the true target. Then you will both automatically advance to the next round of objects and switch roles, so the Listener will become the Speaker and vice versa. There are a total of 60 rounds. After the final round you will fill out a quick 15 second survey and be on your way.

A few final notes: First, since you are playing with another player, you may see this screen before the game begins:

Just hold tight. Another player should join the game within the next few minutes. If you're in the waiting room 15 minutes without another player joining, please email us to let us know (rxdh@stanford.edu).

Second, please be respectful of the worker you're playing with: pay close attention and try to be as responsive as possible. Please do not take notes -- just use your mind!

Finally, please DO NOT refresh the page at any point once you start the game (including the waiting page). If you refresh, you will not be able to rejoin the game you were in and you may not be able to submit the HIT.
Thank you!
